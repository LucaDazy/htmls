💡 Anatomy 101 Primer: What do these terms mean?
Landmark: A distinct physical feature on a bone (like a bump, hole, or ridge) used as a reference point for where muscles attach or nerves pass through.

Origin: The anchor point. This is the end of the muscle attached to a bone that does not move when the muscle contracts.

Insertion: The pulling point. This is the end of the muscle attached to the bone that moves. When a muscle contracts, the insertion is pulled toward the origin.

Action: The actual physical movement the muscle performs (e.g., flexing the arm, closing the jaw).
