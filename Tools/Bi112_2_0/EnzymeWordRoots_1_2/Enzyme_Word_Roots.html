Lab 9 Answer Key: Word Roots & Meanings

Enzyme Name Word Root Meaning Enzyme Function
Polymerase Polymer- (many parts) Synthesizes long chains (polymers) like DNA or RNA from monomers.
Lipase Lip- (fat/lipid) Breaks down lipids (fats) into fatty acids and glycerol.
Ligase Lig- (to bind/tie) Functions to bind or join two molecules together.
Hydrolase Hydro- (water) Breaks chemical bonds by adding water (hydrolysis).
Amylase Amyl- (starch) Breaks down complex starches (amylose) into simple sugars.
Lactase Lact- (milk) Breaks down lactose (milk sugar) into glucose and galactose.
Urease Urea- (urine/urea) Breaks down urea into ammonia and carbon dioxide.
Kinase Kin- (movement/motion) Transfers (moves) phosphate groups from ATP to a target molecule.
Catalase Catal- (break down) Breaks down harmful hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen.
Pepsin Pep- (digestion) Breaks down proteins into smaller peptides during stomach digestion.
Phosphorylase Phosphor- (phosphorus) Adds an inorganic phosphate group to a molecule.
Trypsin Tryp- (to rub/friction) Breaks down proteins in the small intestine.
ATP Synthase Synth- (to make) Synthesizes (makes) ATP from ADP and inorganic phosphate.
